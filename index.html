Building Next Generation Learning at Duke

Collaborative research on learning innovation at Duke University. My teammates and I applied open design methodology as we gathered qualitative insights, ideated concepts, and prototyped solutions in the Duke learning community. An interdisciplinary project within the Duke Bass Connections Program.

Fairness in Context

Decision science research project: "Fairness in context: Comparing allocations in Dictator Games with experiences vs money". In this project I designed and programmed pilot studies in Qualtrics, collected, analyzed, visualized quantitative data, and communicated findings to Duke faculty and scholars. Supported by the Huettel Lab and Duke Department of Psychology & Neuroscience.

Topography of Imagined Realms

Edo-period Japanese art exhibit curation, inspired by the sociocultural background and the philosophy reflected in Edo ukiyo-e. In this iterative design process, I conducted qualitative research on primary visual material, exhibition catalogues, and scholarly articles. Completed as the final project of an art history and visual media studies course.
